How to
Light A Fire.

You Will Need

A bundle of kindling
Ignition source
Small twigs
Larger twigs
Logs

Stage 1 - Preperation

Ensure you have enough kindling. You need enough so that if you were to enclose it between both your thumbs and index fingers it would completely fill this circle.
If you are using a wood source and it is wet then strip the bark from the wood.
Ensure you have collected all the pre-requisite items and lay them out close to you in a methodical manner - small to large.

Stage 2 - Ignition

Lay your kindling down, and spread it so that there will be plenty of airflow amongst the material. Then take your lighter, or flint or match and attempt to provoke a flame. Once it catches then gently seperate the material and allow the temperature to build. Once you are sure the flame will not die within 5-10 seconds then grab your small twigs.

Stage 3 - Building The fire.

Hold a bundle of the smaller twigs above the flame by about 5-10cm - in the heat of the small fire. Ensure these twigs are gently spaced apart to allow air to flow freely. The key is to be gentle, smooth and quick. If this stage fails then you will need to start again from stage 1. gather even more material to give you more time to get a decent flame.

Stage 4 - Mother The Fire

Once you have small twigs alight then introduce larger and larger pieces until you have logs alight. Time must be taken to ensure the fire has life and strength. If you look after a fire then it will look after you.
